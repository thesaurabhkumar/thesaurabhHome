Project Description

Radar cross section is the measure of a target's ability to reflect radar signals in the direction of the radar receiver, i.e. it is a measure of the ratio of backscatter power per steradian (unit solid angle) in the direction of the radar (from the target) to the power density that is intercepted by the target. The RCS of a target can be viewed as a comparison of the strength of the reflected signal from a target to the reflected signal from a perfectly smooth sphere of cross sectional area of 1 meter square.

This project can be broadly divided into four main sections :

  • Taking the input of a target and converting the 3ds input into usable format ( a configuration file ) in our case.
  • Electromagnetic Simulation of the complete scenario and obtain the complete set of returned scattered radiations through rigorous calculations. We have and are working to parallelize this module to its fullest. I have described this portion later.
  • Performing the inverse fourier transforms upon the outputs of previous steps so as to obtain the two dimensional data sets for plotting the wireframe model of the target yet detected. This module has not yet been completed and we are looking for various alternatives to do this .
  • Performing the comparison of our obtained model with the wireframes of various standard targets stored in our database. this involves rigorous Image processing algorithms and has yet not been implemented.
